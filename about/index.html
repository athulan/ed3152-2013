About the Course

High precision parts – ranging from semiconductor devices with nanometer features to optical lenses several meters in diameter – are being increasingly used in diverse applications. Precision engineering is necessary for developing the manufacturing processes and designing the manufacturing equipment for these parts. This course focuses on the fundamentals of precision processes and systems, with an emphasis on the design and performance of precision machines tools and systems, metrology, tooling elements, machine structures, sources of error, manufacturing processes, and process planning. The course also discusses the sustainable manufacturing requirements and impacts of precision processes and systems.

Topic Areas: This course will cover (but is not limited to):

  • Machine tool elements and structure,
  • Sources of error: thermal, static, dynamic, process related
  • Precision machining processes and process models
  • Sensors for process monitoring and control
  • Metrology
  • Machine design case studies
  • Examples of precision component manufacture
  • Process planning and design
  • Energy efficiency of precision processes
  • Data-driven manufacturing

Instructor: Dr. Athulan Vijayaraghavan.

Course Material

The course material has been developed based on material from ME 220: Precision Manufacturing that is offered at the University of California, Berkeley. Much of the material has been developed based on the notes and lectures of Prof. David Dornfeld and based on the excellent text, Precision Manufacturing.
